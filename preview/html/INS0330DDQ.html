History

Foundation

Date of foundation: 1733-1746

This church was founded by ʾǝtege Mǝntǝwwāb over a period of 13 years: she began the process in 1733, dedicated the tabot in 1740, completed the building around 1743 and completed the church's endowment sometime after 1746.

History

After its foundation by ʾǝtege Mǝntǝwwāb, Dabra Ḍaḥay Qʷǝsqʷām became one of the most important churches of Gondar. However, it was sacked by ʾaṣe Tewodros II in 1866. He removed many of its manuscripts that ended up in the No record for Institution INS0001BL. The church was sacked even further by the Mahdists in the 1880s and seriously damaged by air strikes in 1941. Although it was recently restored, it lost its past glory.

Other information

Dabra Ḍaḥay Qʷǝsqʷām is a church in the north-western part of Gondar and close to ʾAbbā ʾƎnṭonǝs. It was one of the most important churches of the town and richly endowed with lands in Bāǧanā, Balasā and in ʾƎbǝnnāt that supported 260 clergy.
