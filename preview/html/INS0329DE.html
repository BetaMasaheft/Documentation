History

Foundation

Date of foundation: 15th cent.

Although it is said to have been founded by Saint Ḫiruta ʾAmlāk (13th cent.), allegedly sent there by its spiritual father ʾabbā ʾIyasus Moʾa, this is now believed to be a historical reconstruction linked to the attempts by the networks of Dabra Ḥayq ʾƎsṭifānos and Dabra Libānos to take possession of this monastery. There is in fact no information on the monastery's history before the 15th cent.

History

During the 15th cent., the monastic life expanded at Dāgā ʾƎsṭifānos and the monastery benefitted from land grants, royal burials and artistic realizations. During the second half of the 16th cent., the monastery was still used as a mausoleum for the members of the royal family and seems to have been very prosperous. Between 1563 and 1574, mamhǝr Gabra Krǝstos restored the church with the support of kantibā Gabra Mikāʾel. The monastery supported ʾaṣe Śarṣa Dǝngǝl in the wars against the Muslims and, in turn, received lands in Daq. At the end of 1646, ʾaṣe Fāsiladas gave the monastery lands in Dǝrdǝrā and, later, in Zǝbǝd. In 1646, he also decided to rebuild the church, which was completed between 1651 and 1653. However, the church burnt down again in 1714. Following the foundation of Nārgā Śǝllāse by Bǝrhan Mogasā, some of the lands traditionally attributed to Dāgā ʾƎsṭifānos were given to the new monastery, causing conflicts. The church was burnt down again in approx. 1880? and reconstructed with the present octagonal building.

Other information

The monastery of Dāgā ʾƎsṭifānos is located on the small island of Dāgā, on the eastern side of the main island of Daq at the centre of lake Ṭānā. The history of the monastery and of its monks can be broadly reconstructed thanks to its important library of manuscripts: 67 were microfilmed by Ernst Hammerschmidt and catalogued by Six; approx. 40 were microfilmed by the EMML project and 28 are at the Bibliothèque Nationale de France.
