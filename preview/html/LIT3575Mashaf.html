General description

The Maṣḥafa saʿātāt by Giyorgis of Saglā is a version of the Maṣḥafa saʿātāt , a Church service made up of psalms, biblical readings and hymns. It eventually replaced Saʿātāt za-gǝbṣ.

The three other types of Maṣḥafa saʿātāt identified by Taft 1986, page 266 are:

Saʿātāt za-gǝbṣ: the Coptic Saʿātāt;

a Saʿātāt that includes the entire Psalter in the daily pensum, cp. ;

Maṣḥafa saʿātāt: a Saʿātāt that includes the entire Psalter in the daily pensum;

Gize ṣalot: a cursus of prayers which is not really a Saʿātāt, but a Gize ṣalot for hermits and recluses.
