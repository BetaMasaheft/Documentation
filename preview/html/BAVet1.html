    In this manuscript there are 1 Invocation, 1 ScribalNoteCompleting, 1 Record, 1 CalendaricNote and 1 Unclear.

    1. (Type: Invocation)

      (ar)

      (en) "Remember, o my Lord, your servant, the poor sinner priest abriyl the Egyptian, delegate of the Alexandrine patriarch and monk of the Monastery of the Virgin Mary of abal al-ayr; and remember, o Lord, those who will commemorate us amongst the Ethiopian fathers".

      The note is written in Arabic on the front board on its inner side. It was drawn up by the Egyptian priest **No record for Person PRS10777Gabriyal** = abriyl **.

      (Type: ScribalNoteCompleting)

      (gez) (sic) () (sic) (sic)

      (Type: Record)

      (gez)

Be careful o reader, that this codex is very defective and its reliability is of low value". Note written by a later librarian and by Johann-Michael Wansleben concerning the reliability of the manuscript. The note was probably written in 1665.
    2. f. 1r Etiopico. 19th century. Note written by a later librarian.
    3. f. 1r
    4. f. 11r Two lines containing the incipit of **No record for Work LIT1349Epistl** = Letter of Eusebius to Carpianus **, as in lines 3 - 7 .
    5. f. 99r Officium Abissinorum. Note written on the upper margin by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.
    6. f. 147r Incipiunt Canones S. Clementis. Note written on the upper margin by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.
    7. f. 164r Concil. Ancyranum. Note written on the upper margin by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.
    8. f. 168r Concil. Ga(n)gerense. Note written on the upper margin by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.
    9. f. 147r Nota b(ene) Maximum hic esse errore. confundit et miscet enim hic duo Concilia. Stulte idem error et jam est in Cod(ice) Magni Duc(is) Florentiae. Sed Cod(ex) Abiss(inoru)m et meus bene habent o(mn)ia notauit a(utem) Fr(ater) G(iovanni) M(ichahel) Vanslebius Ord. Praed. "Nota bene: a serious error is here, as (the scribe) confuses and blends here two councils. The same error is also trivially attested in the manuscript of the Grand Duke of Florence (i.e, **No record for Manuscript BMLor148** (mss) ). However, the manuscript of the Ethiopians (i.e., of San Stefano dei Mori, (i.e, **No record for Manuscript BAVborget2** (mss) ) and mine display everything correctly. Brother Johann Michael Wansleben annotated (this)". Note written in the intercolum by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.
    10. f. 172r Concilii Laodiceni canones. Note written on the outer upper margin by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.
    11. f. 173r Concil. Nicaenum. Note written on the outer upper margin by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.

    14. f. 187v Canones Petri Per S. Clementem. Note written on the outer margin by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.
    15. f. 192r Concil. Antiochaenum. Note written on the outer margin by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.
    16. f. 195v Concil. Laodicaenum. Note written on the outer margin by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.
    17. f. 197v Aduerte Lector huc referri debere illos Canones quos scriptor supra ad conciliu(m) Gangerense annexuit. "Be careful o reader, that here you have to refer to those canons that the scribe previously appended to the council of Gangra". Note written by Johann-Michael Wansleben probably in 1665.

    19. f. 217r Doodles.

    21. Quires are numbered with Arabic numerals on the lower outer margin of the first folio of each quire. Most of them are abraded.
