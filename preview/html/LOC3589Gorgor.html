Other information

Gorgorā is a peninsula on the north of the LakeṬānā. It is 12 km wide in its southern part and stands on a strategical location, an elevated rocky promontory overlooking the lake and next to a series of islands suitable for providing shelter. The peninsula is located in today's ʾAmhārā Regional State while once formed part of the of the Dambǝyā province. Gorgorā gained its prominence under the Jesuits as a missionary residence and a major Catholic church thanks to a land grant offered by ʾaṣe Susǝnyos in 1607. In 1611, Susǝnyos established a katamā in Gorgorā and in 1621 he asked the Jesuits to build a royal palace to the south-west, in an area that became known as New Gorgorā. After the restauration of Orthodoxy, New Gorgorā was the first residence to be abandoned by the missionaries and, in 1665, ʾaṣe Fāsiladas ordered the burning of the "books of Franks". The Italians seized the Gorgorā peninsula on 14 April 1936 and in 1938 inaugurated the Faro della Conquistaon the Vetta Mussolini.
