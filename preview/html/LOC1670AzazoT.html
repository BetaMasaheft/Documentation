History

Foundation

Date of foundation: 17th-18th century

Local priests believe that it was built by ʾaṣe Fāsiladas in the 17th century, while, according to James Bruce, it was built by Iyasu II which would mean it was built only in the 18th century.

History

ʾAzazo Takla Hāymānot is one of the so-called 44 churches of Gondar. It is located in the town of ʾAzazo, 13 km southwest of Gondar. This church played an important role in the history of Ethiopian monasticism: following the burning of Dabra Libānos in 1532 and the decision to move the monastic community north in the lake Ṭānā area, the monastic community of Dabra Libānos finally settled in ʾAzazo.

ʾAzazo Takla Hāymānot is located at the centre of a hilltop, surrounded by a courtyard and a circular stone wall with a square tower.
