Foundation

Date of foundation: 17th century

Although the name "Gondar" is first mentioned in the 14th century to designate a regiment of soldiers and it was subsequently used to designate a small market town inhabited by Muslim traders and Beta ʾƎsrāʾel RELS craftsmen, it developed as a major centre only after ʾaṣe Fāsiladas RELS chose it as his camp in 1636-1637.

History

The town of Gondar is located in today's North Gondar RELS Zone (historically Bagemdǝr RELS province) in the ʾAmhārā RELS Regional State. Gondar started to flourish after ʾaṣe Fāsiladas RELS chose it as his camp in 1636-'37 to control the collection of customs on the Red Sea RELS - Ṭānā RELS - ʾƎnnāryā RELS trade routes. Following its foundation and for approx. 130 years (from 1630s to 1760s), Gondar was the political, religious, cultural and intellectual capital of the Gondarine Kingdom RELS . Its founder Fāsiladas RELS built the first nucleus of the town including his castle, some churches and his pleasure pool. After him, his son ʾaṣe Yoḥānnǝs I RELS and grandson ʾaṣe ʾIyāsu I RELS chose to remain in Gondar and built their own castles and churches. So by the late 17th century, Gondar had become a permanent and sizeable settlement where Ethiopian rulers resided when not campaigning and where the highest commerical, political and religious classes gathered around the throne. In the 17th century Gondar also became the major commercial centre of northern Ethiopia RELS . During the 18th century, the royal compound was completed with the construction of castles by Dāwit III RELS , Bakāffā RELS and ʾIyāsu II RELS , the church of ʾAṭṭāṭāmi Mikāʾel RELS by Dāwit III RELS and various subsidiary buildings (the whole surrounded by a double wall with twelve gates and reinforcing towers). However, by the late 18th century, no more castles were built and, from the 19th century, Gondar gradually lost its political power and shrank in size while the centre of the power moved to Šawā RELS . Tewodros II RELS set his capital in Dabra Tābor RELS and, in 1866 attacked, burnt and looted Gondar. Finally, in 1888 and 1889, the Mahdists attacked and burnt down what was left of the city which saw some redevelopment only in the 1920s . During the Italian occupation, Gondar became the capital of the Provincia di Amhara RELS (one of six provinces forming the Africa Orientale Italiana RELS ), it was restructured and rebuilt along modernist lines and its population increased significantly. After the defeat of the Italians in 1941, Gondar became the capital of the Province of Bagemdǝr RELS and Sǝmen RELS , flourished and grew in population becoming a major commercial and cultural centre.

Other information

Gondar has enjoyed a strategic position: it is 35 km north of the lake Ṭānā RELS close to the point where two strategic caravan routes joined. It also benefitted from being surrounded by a semi-cyrcle of mountains and close to fertile lands and water supply. Historically, Gondar population was divided into two main groups that lived in clearly separated quarters and neighbourhoods: the royal court and nobility on the one hand; the merchants, craftsmen and commoners on the other. Until the Italian occupation, Muslims and Beta ʾƎsrāʾel RELS had to live separated from the Christians and were assigned precise quarters. Gondar is today one of the largest cities in Ethiopia RELS .
